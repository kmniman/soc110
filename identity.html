Interactive Identity Map

Use this activity to map how socialization, group membership, and roles shape your identity. This builds on Chapters 4–6 of your sociology textbook.

1. Agents of Socialization

Agents of socialization are people and institutions that influence your social development, such as family, friends, school, media, and religion (Chapter 5).

2. Role Expectations

A role is the expected behavior attached to a status (Chapter 4). Ascribed roles are assigned at birth (e.g., sibling), while achieved roles are earned (e.g., student or employee).

Role strain happens when it's hard to meet expectations within one role. Role conflict happens when expectations from different roles clash.

3. Group Affiliations

Groups are collections of people with shared norms and interactions (Chapter 6). Primary groups are close and personal (e.g., family), while secondary groups are larger and goal-oriented (e.g., class, workplace). Reference groups are those you compare yourself to, and out-groups are those you don’t identify with.
